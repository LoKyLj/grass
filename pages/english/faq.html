Caring for artificial turf is relatively simple, and adhering to a few basic rules will help keep it lush and vibrant.

  1. Regular brushing: Use a stiff brush to comb the grass against its natural structure. This helps prevent matting and keeps the fibers in an upright position. The brush also helps evenly distribute the infill, ensuring a uniform appearance.
  2. Debris cleanup: Regularly remove leaves, branches, and other debris from the surface of the artificial turf. Use a leaf blower or manually collect large particles. Prompt debris cleanup prevents the decomposition of organic matter on the surface and helps maintain a clean appearance.
  3. Food and spill cleanup: Immediately clean up accidentally spilled food, drinks, or pet waste. Use mild soap or a recommended artificial turf cleaning solution with water to gently wipe the affected area. Rinse thoroughly with water to remove any residues. For pet stains, use a gentle pet-safe disinfectant.
  4. Weed control: Although artificial turf is resistant to weeds, occasionally, weeds may sprout along the edges or through drainage holes. Remove emerging weeds manually or use a weed-killing solution that is safe for artificial turf.
  5. Check the drainage system: Periodically inspect the drainage system and clear it of debris that may accumulate in the drainage holes.
  6. Avoid aggressive chemicals: Refrain from using aggressive chemicals, solvents, or strong cleaning agents on artificial turf. Stick to mild soap or specially formulated artificial turf cleaning solutions.
  7. Seasonal maintenance: Conduct a deep cleaning at least once a year, especially in high-traffic areas. You can use a pressure washer or hire our professional artificial turf cleaning team.

By following these simple care methods, you can ensure the longevity and aesthetic appeal of your artificial turf carpet, providing you with a lush and green landscape all year round.

Yes, you can place furniture or pots with plants on the surface of artificial turf. However, there are several important points to consider:

  1. Weight Distribution: If you are placing heavy items such as furniture on artificial turf, ensure that the weight is evenly distributed to prevent deformation or fiber compression.
  2. Trays for Pots: If you have pots with plants, it's recommended to use trays or stands under them. This helps prevent water accumulation under the pots, which could lead to drainage issues.
  3. Moving Items: Periodically move furniture and pots to avoid water pooling in specific areas and to evenly distribute the load on the surface.
